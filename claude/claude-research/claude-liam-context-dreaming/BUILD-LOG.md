## Decisions

1. **Seven acts, matching the brief's arc one-to-one.** The talk's argument
   is genuinely seven-staged; merging acts would have buried either the
   production principles or the ceiling. Act cards carry the wayfinding load.
2. **Bookshelf analogy split across lanes.** Brief requires the analogy be
   rebuilt as a concept illustration, not narrated over wallpaper: vox run R1
   (B09–B10) carries the mood and the human analogy; B11 (Remotion,
   shelf-of-spines ChipGrid adaptation with a context meter) carries the
   mechanism. Two beats of texture, one beat of machinery.
3. **"Optimistic locking" named as ours, then grounded in her Q&A.** B20
   names the pattern (the brief instructs this); B21 immediately shows the
   verbatim Q&A exchange so the databases admission is hers on record —
   the episode never claims she used the term herself.
4. **All results claims speaker-reported.** B24 and B40 stamp a persistent
   "speaker-reported" tag on screen and keep the framing in the voice and in
   the B42 recap ("she reports cost runs downhill"). The transcript has no
   numbers, so the episode shows none.
5. **Datable content stripped from narration.** No "Claude Managed agents",
   no "memory and dreaming API", no product CTA anywhere in narration (her
   own Q&A: she couldn't make product CTAs). The talk's pointer survives only
   as a one-line paraphrase on the B41 credit card and in SOURCES.md, per the
   brief's allowance.
6. **Tier 3 speaker.** No stills or generated imagery of Lamis Mukta,
   anywhere, ever. Named in B00 narration (framing) and on the B41 credit
   card (name, role, talk title, event, URL). All vox subjects are Tier 1
   generic objects from the brief's approved list.
7. **One ask→result pair, at the centerpiece.** B34 (composer micro-beat,
   exempt) asks Claude to draw the dreaming pipeline exactly as the talk
   describes it; B35–B36 are the result. Mirrors the exemplar's single
   mid-reel ask; keeps ILLUSTRATE LAW intact everywhere else.
8. **Act-III wounds replayed and healed in Act IV.** B20's mechanism catches
   the B14 collision on screen; B22's RO badge bounces the B15 poison write.
   Callbacks make the principles answers, not a list.
9. **Green exam-hall clock kept, run R2 kept to 2 beats.** The clock is a
   standalone drawon vox beat (B33, radians→degrees) rather than a third run
   beat — a 3-beat run would have forced the camera past what one exam-hall
   plate supports.
10. **Transcription artifacts resolved conservatively** ("ripples" →
    re-reads; "subjects" → subagents; "linings" → learnings) — each logged in
    SOURCES.md and FACTCHECK.md; no verbatim quote drawn from a garbled span.

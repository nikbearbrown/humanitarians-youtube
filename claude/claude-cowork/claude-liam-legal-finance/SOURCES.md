## TWO plugins, one chapter (the structural fact-check note)
Per the book-level **FACTCHECK.md, claim #6:** **Legal and Finance are two SEPARATE official plugins** in Anthropic's `knowledge-work-plugins` repo. The book merges them into a single "Legal and Finance" chapter, which undercuts its own "one chapter per plugin" promise. For a reel this merge is fine — they share the "protect the solo operator" pattern and make one coherent episode — but the reel must **never imply a single combined plugin.**

How this reel handles it:
- Narration and cards always say **"the legal plugin"** and **"the finance plugin"** as distinct tools; B00 opens on "Two plugins," and the verdict card's first line reads **"Two separate plugins, one job."**
- Legal and finance get **their own acts** (II+III legal; IV+V finance) rather than being blended.
- No beat ever names a "Legal and Finance plugin" as one object. The subtitle "The Legal and Finance Plugins" is plural by design.

## Claims carried (verified true / evergreen mechanism)
- The legal plugin is a **screening tool**: reviews documents against risk frameworks, color-coded flags, NDA/vendor/compliance review, redline suggestions. It does **not** give legal advice, weigh the deal strategically, replace counsel for significant matters, or guarantee a legally sound read. ✓
- The finance plugin is an **analysis accelerator**: budget/variance, forecasting, expense trends, simple modeling, scenario modeling (break-even, runway, payback), report generation. It does **not** file taxes, give accounting advice, replace a bookkeeper for complex records, or guarantee regulatory accuracy. ✓
- Both are habit tools — routine screening/review, escalate red flags to a professional, keep documents organized. ✓
- The consequential decision, and any real-stakes sign-off, stays with a human (a real lawyer / accountant). ✓
